# Product

## Register

product

## Users

Currently a single user (James) training solo, mid-workout, under time pressure, one-handed on a
phone between sets. The app is meant to eventually extend to other users, but the core job stays
identical: log sets fast, trust the numbers, get an honest read on progress. Design should serve
the training session, not compete with it for attention.

## Product Purpose

An offline-first personal training log and coach engine. It builds a training week, prescribes
weights with a plain-language reason for every number, tracks per-set logging (reps, weight, RPE),
and gives an honest read on progress including when it goes backwards. No backend, no accounts,
state lives in localStorage. Success = the user trusts the numbers enough to stop thinking about
the app and just train.

## Brand Personality

Blunt, honest, instrument-like. Dark, angular, zero-radius. No motivational-poster energy, no fake
praise, no gamified streak-shaming. Every prescribed number carries its real reason. When a session
goes backwards, the app says so plainly rather than dressing it up.

## Anti-references

Generic fitness-influencer apps: gradient hero cards, badge/streak-shaming, rounded-friendly SaaS
softness, hype copy ("CRUSH IT", "You're on fire!"), decorative motivational photography. Nothing
here should read as trying to sell excitement — it should read as a precise tool.

## Design Principles

- **The why is the product.** Every prescribed weight, every selection, every regression carries a
  plain-language reason. Never strip the rationale to save space.
- **Honesty over encouragement.** Never fake praise or paper over a bad session. State it plainly.
- **One accent, spent deliberately.** Orange marks exactly one action per screen — the thing to do
  next — never decoration.
- **Quiet by default, loud only where it matters.** Status is peripheral (stroke-check, hairline
  circle); the one thing demanding action each screen is the only loud element.
- **Speed for the compliant path.** Logging the plan as written should take one tap; deviation is
  the edit, not the default flow.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

No specific accommodations raised yet; hold to WCAG AA contrast as a baseline given real-world use
(gym lighting, tired eyes mid-workout, one-handed operation).
